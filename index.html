Three critical checks — done in one visit — that protect your system before peak cooling season starts.

Wash Coils

Dirty coils force your system to overwork — raising your electric bill and shortening equipment life. A thorough coil wash restores peak airflow and efficiency.

  • Better cooling performance
  • Lower energy bills
  • Extended equipment life
Most Critical

Check Refrigerant Charge

Low refrigerant is the #1 reason A/C units stop cooling. Catching a shortfall before the heat peaks saves you from an emergency no-cool call in the middle of summer.

  • Verify proper charge levels
  • Catch leaks early
  • Prevent emergency repairs

Check Capacitor

Capacitors are one of the most common warm-weather failure points. A weak capacitor causes hard starts, increased energy draw, and eventual compressor damage — caught early, it's a cheap fix.

  • Prevent compressor failure
  • Avoid hard starts
  • Catch problems before breakdown

Common Questions

Got Questions? We've Got Answers.

What exactly is included in the $150 tune-up?

The $150 Spring A/C Tune-Up includes three core services: coil washing (removes debris that forces your system to work harder), refrigerant charge check (ensures your system is properly charged and catches leaks early), and capacitor inspection (one of the most common summer failure points). All three are performed in a single visit.

What areas does Dragon A/C service?

Dragon A/C LLC serves North Texas including the DFW metroplex and surrounding areas. Call 214-384-4420 to confirm your address is in the service area before scheduling.

How long does the tune-up take?

Most tune-ups take 60–90 minutes. Jeremy will give you a heads-up on-site if anything found during the inspection could extend the visit — always before any additional work is started.

Is $150 really the final price? Are there hidden fees?

$150 is the flat price for the first unit — all three checks included. If your system needs additional refrigerant, a replacement part, or any extra work, Jeremy will quote you the cost before touching anything additional. No surprise bills, ever.

Is Dragon A/C licensed and insured?

Yes. Dragon A/C LLC holds license TACLA98821E — a valid Texas A/C and Refrigeration Contractor license. Jeremy Dragon is a fully licensed and insured HVAC professional with North Texas experience.

My A/C seems fine — do I really need a tune-up?

Most A/C problems don't show symptoms until they cause a full breakdown — often at the worst possible time (peak summer heat). A tune-up catches small issues like a weakening capacitor or slightly low refrigerant charge before they become emergency failures. Prevention is always significantly cheaper than emergency repair plus the cost of a 100°F house.
